About the FOOD Center

Driving Competitiveness Across the Entire Value Chain

The FOOD Center is being designed to drive competitiveness and affordability across the entire food and beverage value chain — from field and farm to factory, distribution, and retail. It brings together producers, manufacturers, distributors, retailers, original equipment manufacturers (OEMs), and trade associations to identify where Department of Energy investments in research and development can have the greatest national impact.

These insights will directly inform the formation of a multi-million-dollar national R&D center focused on helping U.S. companies produce more food and beverage products at lower cost for American consumers.
Value Chain Coverage

Food & Beverage Value Chain Coverage

The FOOD Center takes a holistic view of the entire food and beverage system. Unlike initiatives focused on a single sub-sector, the Center is designed to examine the full value chain, capturing not only what happens on the farm or on the processing floor, but also how products move, are stored, and reach consumers.

Agriculture

Crop production, livestock, dairy, aquaculture, and related farm-level processes. Topics include precision agriculture, farm automation, robotics, water and land use productivity, and energy intensity on the farm.

Food Processing

Industrial operations that transform raw agricultural inputs into food products. Key processes include drying, washing, heating, cooling, sterilization, fermentation, packaging, and refrigeration.

Transportation & Storage

Cross-cutting logistics that link every node of the value chain. Focus areas include warehouse energy performance, cold storage technology, and fleet-level cost-effectiveness in food transport.

Retail & Distribution

Grocery, foodservice, and direct-to-consumer channels. The Center examines refrigeration, cold chain integrity, store-level energy use, and the connectivity between distribution networks and retail facilities.

Byproducts & Waste Streams

Opportunities to recover value from food production residuals, including biogas, compostables, and material reuse, to improve overall system productivity.

Crosscutting Technologies

Innovations with broad applicability across sub-sectors: AI and machine learning, data sharing platforms, advanced sensors, and digital integration across the value chain.

Your Voice Matters

A Rare Opportunity to Influence Federal Investment

This is a rare opportunity to influence where the Department of Energy invests, what challenges get prioritized, and who is at the table.

The FOOD Center is being developed based on a structured, industry-led customer discovery effort. Rather than arriving with pre-defined solutions, the FOOD Center begins by listening — systematically gathering input from stakeholders across key sub-sectors to build a rigorous, evidence-based picture of where the food and beverage industry most needs support.

1:1 Stakeholder Interviews

Structured conversations with producers, manufacturers, distributors, retailers, OEMs, and trade associations. Interviews are guided by a semi-structured survey and target stakeholders across the U.S., engaging both large multinationals and small operators.

Web-Based Workshops

Facilitated online workshops organized by sub-sector, designed to validate interview and research findings, gather feedback at scale, and refine priorities through collective stakeholder input.

In-Person Site Visits

Targeted in-person conversations and facility tours with small farmers and producers across the U.S., grounding the FOOD Center's priorities in the practical realities of diverse operational contexts.

The industry engagement process will result in a pair of outputs that will shape the FOOD Center's long-term direction and guide future Department of Energy investment.

Deliverables

Shaping the FOOD Center's Direction

FOOD Center Charter

Defines how industry engages with the FOOD Center, including governance structure, steering committees, collaboration mechanisms, and a process for continuously surfacing emerging needs and investment priorities.

FOOD Center Roadmap

A research and development framework that translates stakeholder-identified challenges and technology gaps into a clear plan for future investments, pilot projects, and national-scale deployment.
